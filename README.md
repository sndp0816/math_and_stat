\## Stage 0 — Number Sense \& Arithmetic



> \*Counting, operations, fractions — the atoms of math\*



This is where all math begins. Comfort with numbers — how they're named, how they relate, how operations transform them — underpins everything that follows. Don't rush it.



\*\*Topics:\*\*

\- Counting \& place value

\- Addition \& subtraction

\- Multiplication \& division

\- Fractions \& decimals

\- Percentages \& ratios

\- Negative numbers

\- Order of operations (PEMDAS / BODMAS)

\- Estimation \& rounding



\---



\## Stage 1 — Pre-Algebra \& Basic Geometry



> \*Patterns, unknowns, shapes, and measurement\*



The bridge from arithmetic to formal reasoning. Variables appear for the first time. You also start thinking spatially — angles, area, perimeter — which trains geometric intuition you'll use forever.



\*\*Topics:\*\*

\- Variables \& expressions

\- Equations with one unknown

\- Proportions \& unit conversion

\- Integers on the number line

\- Angles \& triangles

\- Area \& perimeter

\- Coordinate plane basics

\- Intro to data \& statistics



\---



\## Stage 2 — Algebra



> \*Generalise arithmetic — let letters do the work\*



Algebra is the language of mathematics. Mastering it means you can describe any relationship symbolically and manipulate it. Linear, quadratic, exponential — you'll recognise these shapes everywhere.



\*\*Topics:\*\*

\- Linear equations \& inequalities

\- Systems of equations

\- Polynomials \& factoring

\- Quadratic formula

\- Functions \& function notation

\- Exponentials \& logarithms

\- Sequences \& series

\- Intro to complex numbers



\---



\## Stage 3 — Geometry \& Trigonometry



> \*Space, angles, and the power of the unit circle\*



Geometry is how mathematics describes the physical world. Trigonometry extends it to waves, rotations, and oscillations — you'll find it hiding inside every physics formula and signal-processing concept.



\*\*Topics:\*\*

\- Euclidean proofs \& postulates

\- Congruence \& similarity

\- Pythagorean theorem (deep dive)

\- Circles, arcs \& sectors

\- Coordinate geometry

\- Trigonometric ratios

\- Unit circle \& radians

\- Trig identities \& equations



\---



\## Stage 4 — Pre-Calculus \& Analytic Geometry



> \*Preparing the launch pad for calculus\*



This stage builds the function fluency needed for calculus. Limits, an informal sense of continuity, and a library of functions — polynomial, rational, trig, exponential — all assembled and ready to differentiate.



\*\*Topics:\*\*

\- Polynomial \& rational functions

\- Conic sections

\- Parametric \& polar coordinates

\- Limits (informal)

\- Continuity intuition

\- Vectors in 2D \& 3D

\- Binomial theorem

\- Intro to probability



\---



\## Stage 5 — Calculus



> \*The mathematics of change and accumulation\*



Calculus is arguably the biggest leap: from static to dynamic. Derivatives describe change; integrals accumulate it. Together they describe motion, growth, decay, and optimisation — the core toolkit of every physical science.



\*\*Topics:\*\*

\- Limits (formal, ε-δ)

\- Derivatives \& differentiation rules

\- Chain, product \& quotient rules

\- Implicit differentiation

\- Optimisation \& curve sketching

\- Integration \& antiderivatives

\- Fundamental theorem of calculus

\- Integration techniques

\- Series \& Taylor expansions

\- Intro to multivariable calculus

\- Differential equations (ODEs)



\---



\## Stage 6 — Linear Algebra \& Discrete Math



> \*Vectors, matrices, graphs, and the logic of structures\*



Linear algebra is the secret engine inside machine learning, physics, computer graphics, and quantum mechanics. Discrete math is the foundation of computer science. Both train a new way of seeing structure.



\*\*Topics:\*\*

\- Vectors \& vector spaces

\- Matrices \& matrix operations

\- Determinants

\- Eigenvalues \& eigenvectors

\- Linear transformations

\- Graph theory

\- Combinatorics \& counting

\- Proof techniques (induction, contradiction)

\- Number theory basics

\- Boolean algebra \& logic

\- Recurrence relations



\---



\## Stage 7 — Advanced \& Specialised Fields



> \*The frontier — pick your universe\*



At this level, mathematics branches into its many specialisations. Each one connects back to the foundations you've built. Choose the branch that aligns with your goals — and you'll find roads in every direction.



\*\*Topics:\*\*

\- Real analysis

\- Complex analysis

\- Abstract algebra

\- Topology

\- Probability theory \& statistics

\- Partial differential equations

\- Numerical methods

\- Fourier \& Laplace transforms

\- Differential geometry

\- Group \& ring theory

\- Quantum mechanics math

\- Information theory

\- Mathematical logic \& set theory

\- Category theory



\---



\## Tips for the Journey



| Tip | Why it matters |

| :-- | :------------- |

| Don't skip Stage 0 \& 1 | Weak arithmetic shows up as a ceiling in calculus |

| Master algebra deeply | Most calculus struggles are really algebra struggles |

| Calculus is a milestone, not a destination | It opens the door to the real landscape |

| Pick a direction at Stage 7 | Analysis → pure math · Linear algebra + PDEs → physics \& ML · Probability → data science |

| Obsession beats talent | Consistency over years matters more than raw ability |



\---



\## A Note on Greatness



This roadmap gives you the \*\*tools and structure\*\* that even mathematical legends like Ramanujan lacked in formal terms. Completing it puts you at the level of a research mathematician — someone who can discover new things, solve hard problems, and contribute something the world didn't have before.



The roadmap is the door. Curiosity and hard thinking are what's on the other side.
